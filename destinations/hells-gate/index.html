About Hell's Gate National Park

Hell's Gate National Park is one of Kenya's most unique and adventurous destinations, offering visitors the rare opportunity to explore a wildlife park on foot, by bicycle, or on a motorcycle.

Located in the Great Rift Valley, Hell's Gate National Park is named for the narrow break in the cliffs that was once a tributary of a prehistoric lake. The park is characterized by its dramatic landscapes, including towering cliffs, water-gouged gorges, stark rock towers, scrub-clad volcanoes, and belching plumes of geothermal steam.

What makes this park truly special is that it's one of the few parks in Kenya where you can walk or cycle among the wildlife. The park is home to a variety of animals including zebras, giraffes, buffalo, antelopes, and over 100 species of birds. The geothermal activity in the park has also made it a popular location for geothermal energy production, with the Olkaria Geothermal Station located nearby.
