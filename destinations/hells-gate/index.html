About Hell's Gate National Park

Hell's Gate National Park is one of Kenya's most unique and adventurous destinations, offering visitors the rare opportunity to explore a wildlife park on foot, by bicycle, or on a motorcycle.

Located in the Great Rift Valley, Hell's Gate National Park is named for the narrow break in the cliffs that was once a tributary of a prehistoric lake. The park is characterized by its dramatic landscapes, including towering cliffs, water-gouged gorges, stark rock towers, scrub-clad volcanoes, and belching plumes of geothermal steam.

Did you know? Hell's Gate inspired the setting for Disney's The Lion King and is one of the few parks in Kenya where you can walk or cycle among wildlife.

What makes this park truly special is that it's one of the few parks in Kenya where you can walk or cycle among the wildlife. The park is home to a variety of animals including zebras, giraffes, buffalo, antelopes, and over 100 species of birds. The geothermal activity in the park has also made it a popular location for geothermal energy production, with the Olkaria Geothermal Station located nearby.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Great Rift Valley, 90km from Nairobi
  • Size: 68 km² (26 sq miles)
  • Best Time: Year-round, especially July-October
  • Climate: Warm and dry, semi-arid
  • Famous For: Cycling safaris & geothermal activity
  • Access: Drive from Nairobi (1.5 hours)

What Makes Hell's Gate Special

Adventure Activities

  • Cycling Safaris: Bike among wildlife
  • Rock Climbing: Fischer's Tower and Central Tower
  • Hiking: Explore gorges and geothermal areas
  • Walking Safaris: No vehicle restrictions

Geological Wonders

  • Dramatic cliffs and gorges
  • Hot springs and steam vents
  • Unique volcanic formations
  • Stunning Rift Valley views

Wildlife & Birdlife

Mammals

  • Zebras, giraffes, and buffalo
  • Antelopes and warthogs
  • Baboons and monkeys
  • Occasional predators

Birds

  • Over 100 species
  • Vultures and eagles
  • Secretary birds
  • Colorful songbirds

Best Time to Visit

July - October

Dry Season: Best for cycling, hiking, and wildlife viewing. Pleasant weather and clear skies.

Year-Round

Always Open: The park is accessible all year, but some activities are best in the dry season.
