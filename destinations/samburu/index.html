About Samburu National Reserve

Samburu National Reserve is a wildlife haven located in the arid northern region of Kenya, offering visitors the extraordinary experience of seeing unique species found nowhere else in the country.

Did you know?

Samburu is home to the “Samburu Special Five”—rare animals found almost nowhere else in Kenya: the Grevy’s zebra, Somali ostrich, reticulated giraffe, gerenuk, and Beisa oryx.

Situated on the banks of the Ewaso Nyiro River, Samburu National Reserve is characterized by its dramatic landscape of rugged hills, open savannah, and riverine forests. The reserve is home to the "Samburu Special Five" - unique species that are rarely found in other parts of Kenya: the Grevy's zebra, Somali ostrich, reticulated giraffe, gerenuk, and Beisa oryx.

The reserve's location in the northern frontier district gives it a distinct character, with semi-arid conditions creating a unique ecosystem that supports a variety of wildlife adapted to the harsh environment. The Ewaso Nyiro River serves as a lifeline, attracting large herds of elephants, buffalo, and other animals, especially during the dry season.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Northern Kenya, 350km from Nairobi
  • Size: 165 sq km
  • Best Time: June to October (Dry season)
  • Climate: Hot and dry, semi-arid

Wildlife & The Samburu Special Five

The Samburu Special Five

  • Grevy's Zebra: The largest and most endangered zebra species
  • Somali Ostrich: Distinctive blue-grey legs and neck
  • Reticulated Giraffe: Beautiful geometric pattern
  • Gerenuk: Long-necked antelope that stands on hind legs
  • Beisa Oryx: Elegant antelope with long straight horns

Other Wildlife

  • Large elephant herds
  • Lions, leopards, and cheetahs
  • Hippos and crocodiles in the river
  • Over 350 bird species
  • Rare vulturine guineafowl

Safari Activities

  • Morning and evening game drives
  • Photography safaris
  • Cultural visits to local villages
  • Bird watching tours
  • Walking safaris (with guides)

Best Time to Visit

June - October

Dry Season: Best wildlife viewing with animals congregating around water sources. Clear skies and pleasant temperatures.

November - May

Wet Season: Lush green landscapes, bird watching at its best, fewer tourists, and lower rates available.

Year-Round

Always Amazing: The Samburu Special Five can be seen throughout the year. Each season offers unique experiences.
