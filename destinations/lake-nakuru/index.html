About Lake Nakuru National Park

Lake Nakuru National Park is a birdwatcher's paradise and one of Kenya's most beautiful parks, famous for its spectacular flamingo populations and stunning Great Rift Valley scenery.

Located in the Great Rift Valley, Lake Nakuru National Park was established in 1961 and has since become one of Kenya's most popular wildlife destinations. The park is centered around Lake Nakuru, a shallow alkaline lake that provides the perfect feeding ground for thousands of flamingos, creating one of the most iconic wildlife spectacles in Africa.

Did you know? Lake Nakuru is famous for its pink flamingos, which can number in the hundreds of thousands during peak season, creating a breathtaking pink shoreline.

The park's diverse habitats include the lake itself, surrounding grasslands, acacia woodlands, and rocky cliffs. This variety of environments supports an incredible array of wildlife, including both black and white rhinos, lions, leopards, and over 450 species of birds. The park's compact size makes it perfect for day trips or as part of a larger safari itinerary.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Great Rift Valley, 160km from Nairobi
  • Size: 188 km² (73 sq miles)
  • Best Time: June to March (Flamingo season)
  • Climate: Moderate, pleasant year-round
  • Famous For: Flamingos & birdwatching
  • Access: Drive from Nairobi (2.5 hours)

Wildlife & Birdlife

Famous Flamingos

  • Greater Flamingos: Thousands of pink flamingos
  • Lesser Flamingos: Smaller, more numerous species
  • Seasonal Migration: Numbers vary throughout the year
  • Feeding Spectacle: Filter-feeding on algae

Other Wildlife

  • Both black and white rhinos
  • Lions, leopards, and cheetahs
  • Over 450 bird species
  • Giraffes, zebras, and antelopes
  • Hippos and water birds
