About Lamu Island

Lamu Island is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of Kenya's most enchanting destinations, offering a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

Did you know?

Lamu Old Town is the oldest and best-preserved Swahili settlement in East Africa, and the island is completely car-free—donkeys and dhows are the main modes of transport!

Located off the northern coast of Kenya, Lamu Island is the oldest and best-preserved Swahili settlement in East Africa. The island's Old Town, with its narrow winding streets, traditional stone houses, and ornate wooden doors, has remained virtually unchanged for centuries, offering visitors a glimpse into a bygone era.

The island is car-free, with donkeys and dhows (traditional sailing vessels) serving as the primary modes of transportation. This unique characteristic adds to the island's charm and helps preserve its authentic atmosphere. Lamu's pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and warm hospitality make it an ideal destination for those seeking a peaceful retreat away from the modern world.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Lamu Archipelago, Northern Coast
  • Size: 25 km² (9.7 sq miles)
  • Best Time: July to March (Dry season)
  • Climate: Tropical, hot and humid

Best Time to Visit

July - March

Dry Season: Perfect beach weather, ideal for water activities, clear skies and warm temperatures, best time for dhow sailing.

Cultural Events

Festivals: Lamu Cultural Festival (November), Maulid Festival (Islamic calendar), traditional dhow races, Swahili poetry and music.

Year-Round

Always Enchanting: Lamu's timeless beauty and rich culture are available throughout the year, each season offering unique experiences.
