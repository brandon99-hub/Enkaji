About Amboseli National Park

Amboseli National Park is one of Kenya's most iconic safari destinations, renowned for its large elephant herds and spectacular views of Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa's highest peak. Located in southern Kenya near the border with Tanzania, this 392-square-kilometer park offers some of the most breathtaking wildlife photography opportunities in Africa.

Did you know?

Amboseli is one of the best places in Africa to see large herds of elephants up close, often with the snow-capped peak of Mount Kilimanjaro as a breathtaking backdrop.

The park's landscape is dominated by the majestic Mount Kilimanjaro, which provides a stunning backdrop for wildlife viewing. The name "Amboseli" comes from the Maasai word meaning "salty dust," referring to the dry lake bed that was once part of a larger lake system.

Amboseli is particularly famous for its elephant population, which is one of the most studied in the world. The park's elephants are known for their impressive tusks and the close-knit family bonds they display. Visitors can observe these gentle giants in their natural habitat, often with the snow-capped peak of Kilimanjaro in the background.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Kajiado County, Kenya
  • Size: 392 sq km
  • Best Time: June-October, January-February
  • Famous For: Elephants & Kilimanjaro views
  • Access: Flight from Nairobi (45 min)

Wildlife & Scenery

Elephant Paradise

Amboseli is home to over 1,600 elephants, making it one of the best places in Africa to observe these magnificent creatures. The park's elephants are known for their large tusks and complex social structures. Watch as families interact, calves play, and herds move across the plains.

Mount Kilimanjaro

The snow-capped peak of Mount Kilimanjaro provides a stunning backdrop for wildlife photography. At 5,895 meters, it's Africa's highest mountain and creates one of the most iconic safari scenes in the world. The mountain is best viewed in the early morning and late afternoon.

Other Wildlife

Beyond elephants, Amboseli hosts lions, cheetahs, hyenas, buffalo, giraffes, zebras, wildebeest, and over 400 bird species. The park's diverse habitats include open plains, acacia woodlands, swamps, and the dry lake bed, each supporting different wildlife communities.

Best Time to Visit

June - October

Dry Season: Excellent wildlife viewing with clear views of Mount Kilimanjaro. Animals gather around water sources, making them easier to spot.

January - February

Short Dry Season: Good wildlife viewing with fewer tourists. Clear skies provide excellent photography opportunities.

Year-Round

Always Spectacular: Elephants can be seen throughout the year. Each season offers unique experiences and different perspectives of Kilimanjaro.
