About Mount Kenya

Mount Kenya is Africa's second-highest mountain and one of the most beautiful peaks on the continent, offering a unique combination of equatorial glaciers, diverse ecosystems, and stunning alpine scenery.

Rising to 5,199 meters (17,057 feet), Mount Kenya is an extinct volcano that was formed approximately 3 million years ago. The mountain is a UNESCO World Heritage site and features three main peaks: Batian (5,199m), Nelion (5,188m), and Point Lenana (4,985m). The mountain's name comes from the Kikuyu word "Kirinyaga," meaning "mountain of whiteness," referring to its snow-capped peaks.

The mountain's diverse ecosystems range from dense montane forests at the base to alpine meadows, moorlands, and finally the rocky peaks with their permanent glaciers. This variety of habitats supports a rich biodiversity, including unique plant species like giant lobelias and groundsels, as well as wildlife such as elephants, buffalo, and various antelope species.

Climbing Routes & Peaks

Main Peaks

  • Point Lenana (4,985m): Most accessible peak
  • Batian (5,199m): Highest peak, technical climb
  • Nelion (5,188m): Second highest, technical
  • Point John (4,883m): Good acclimatization peak

Popular Routes

  • Sirimon Route (easiest)
  • Naro Moru Route (fastest)
  • Chogoria Route (most scenic)
  • Burguret Route (least used)
